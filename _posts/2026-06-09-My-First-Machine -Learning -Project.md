One of the most challenging yet rewarding experiences of my first semester was working on a machine learning project. Our project focused on developing a Heart Attack Prediction Model, which introduced me to the practical side of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning for the first time.

Before starting this project, I had very limited knowledge of machine learning. Most of the concepts seemed complicated, and I often wondered how computers could make predictions using data. However, as we started working on the project, I gradually began understanding the importance of data, algorithms, and model training.

The project was assigned in groups of two students. Unfortunately, my group partner was unable to attend university for some time due to personal commitments. As a result, our progress became slower than expected, and I started feeling stressed about meeting deadlines. Despite these difficulties, we continued working on the project whenever possible and tried our best to complete each task.

Our model was trained using the Decision Tree algorithm to predict the risk of heart attacks based on medical data. While working on the project, I learned how machine learning models are developed and how healthcare datasets can be used to solve real-world problems. This experience changed my perspective on technology and showed me the practical value of AI.

Throughout the semester, Dr. Bilal Ahmad encouraged students to work on meaningful projects using real-world datasets. His focus on medical applications of machine learning helped me understand how technology can contribute to society. I also explored some of his research work through his <a href="https://scholar.google.com.au/citations?user=8nZ0jVkAAAAJ&hl=en">Google Scholar profile</a>, which further increased my interest in machine learning.

Although the project was difficult at times, it helped me learn many new concepts and improved my confidence. By the end of the semester, I had a much better understanding of machine learning than when I started. Most importantly, this project inspired me to continue exploring AI and data-driven technologies in the future.
