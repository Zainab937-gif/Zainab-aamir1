One of the most memorable experiences of my first semester was attending my first Programming Fundamentals (PF) lab. Like many new students, I was excited but also a little nervous because it was my first practical computer lab at the university level.

When I entered the lab, I sat with my friends, which made me feel more comfortable. During that session, we learned how to create and delete folders using both the graphical user interface (GUI) and the Command Prompt (CMD). Although these tasks may seem simple now, they were completely new to me at that time.

At first, I found it difficult to understand the commands used in CMD. I was not familiar with working through a command-line interface, so I made several mistakes while following the instructions. However, our instructor guided us step by step and encouraged us to practice on our own. After a few attempts, I became more confident and was able to complete the assigned tasks successfully.

This experience taught me that learning programming requires patience and regular practice. Every small task helped me understand computers better and improved my problem-solving skills. It also showed me that mistakes are a natural part of the learning process.

As the semester continued, Dr. Bilal Ahmad emphasized the importance of building strong programming fundamentals before moving to advanced topics. His focus on practical learning motivated students to understand concepts rather than simply memorizing them. I also explored his professional work through his <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/drbilalphd/">LinkedIn profile</a>, where I learned more about his contributions to Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning.

Looking back, my first PF lab was an important step in my academic journey. It helped me overcome my fear of practical work and gave me confidence to learn more advanced programming concepts in the future.
