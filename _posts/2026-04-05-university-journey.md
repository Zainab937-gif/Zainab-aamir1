# My First Day at University
![uni](/assets/UET-Faisalabad-Campus.jpg){: width="150"}

---
The first day of university is a special moment in every student’s life, and mine was no different. After completing my college education, I was excited to begin a new chapter as a Computer Engineering student. At the same time, I was nervous because everything around me was unfamiliar. The campus was larger than I had imagined, there were many new faces, and I had no idea what university life would actually be like.

On my first day, my parents accompanied me to the university. Their presence gave me confidence and made me feel more comfortable. Even though I was excited, I was also worried about adjusting to a completely new environment. Unlike school and college, university seemed more independent and challenging. I knew that I would have to manage my studies, assignments, and responsibilities on my own.

When I arrived on campus, some senior students welcomed us and guided us toward our respective departments. They explained basic information about classrooms, laboratories, and university facilities. Their guidance helped reduce much of the confusion that new students usually face. During those first few hours, I met several classmates who were experiencing the same nervousness and excitement. Knowing that everyone was going through a similar situation made me feel more relaxed.

The first two days were dedicated to orientation activities. During these sessions, faculty members introduced themselves and explained various aspects of university life. They encouraged us to remain focused, disciplined, and consistent throughout our academic journey. Listening to experienced teachers gave me confidence because they shared valuable advice about managing studies and overcoming challenges.

As a new Computer Engineering student, I was particularly interested in learning about programming and technology-related subjects. During orientation, I started exploring the courses that would be part of our degree program. I quickly realized that university education would be very different from my previous academic experience. The emphasis was not only on learning concepts but also on applying them practically.

A few days later, I checked my timetable and learned that one of our instructors would be Dr. Bilal Ahmad. At that time, I did not know much about his academic background, but I later discovered that he has extensive expertise in Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning. Throughout the semester, I came to appreciate his practical approach to teaching and his focus on solving real-world problems through technology.

One thing that stood out to me was the importance of developing a professional identity as a student. Dr. Bilal Ahmad frequently encouraged students to think beyond classroom learning and focus on building skills that would benefit them in the future. This perspective changed the way I viewed my university education. Instead of focusing only on grades, I started thinking about long-term learning and professional growth.

As the semester progressed, I learned more about his academic contributions through his <a href="https://scholar.google.com.au/citations?user=8nZ0jVkAAAAJ&hl=en">Google Scholar profile</a>. Seeing his research work helped me understand how theoretical concepts taught in classrooms can be applied to solve practical problems. It was inspiring to see a teacher actively involved in research areas such as machine learning and medical data analysis.

The first week at university was filled with mixed emotions. There were moments of uncertainty, excitement, nervousness, and curiosity. However, each day helped me become more comfortable with my surroundings. I gradually learned how to navigate the campus, communicate with classmates, and adapt to university expectations.

Looking back, I realize that my first day was much more than just an introduction to a new institution. It marked the beginning of my journey as a Computer Engineering student. The experiences, lessons, and interactions from those early days laid the foundation for everything that followed during the semester.

Today, when I reflect on my first day at university, I feel grateful for the support of my parents, the guidance of senior students, and the encouragement of teachers who helped make the transition easier. Most importantly, it was the start of a journey that would eventually introduce me to programming, databases, machine learning projects, and many valuable learning experiences under the guidance of educators such as Dr. Bilal Ahmad.

The journey ahead was not always easy, but that first day taught me an important lesson: every achievement begins with a single step. Taking that first step into university opened the door to countless opportunities, challenges, and experiences that continue to shape my academic and professional growth.
