After selecting a suitable dataset for our Thyroid Cancer Recurrence Prediction Project, the next step was designing the database. Before creating any machine learning model, we needed a structured way to store and manage patient data. This stage helped me understand the importance of database design in real-world applications.

We started by analyzing the dataset and identifying important entities and attributes. Using this information, we created an Entity Relationship (ER) Diagram to visualize the structure of our database. The ER diagram helped us understand how different pieces of information were connected and how relationships could be established between tables.

After completing the ER diagram, we implemented the database in MySQL. We created tables and defined Primary Keys and Foreign Keys to maintain data integrity. We also practiced SQL queries for inserting, updating, and retrieving data. This practical experience improved my understanding of database management systems and showed me how databases support machine learning applications.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad explained that a well-designed database is the foundation of any successful project. His emphasis on proper database design helped us appreciate the importance of organizing data before applying machine learning techniques. I also explored his <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/drbilalphd/">LinkedIn profile</a> to learn more about his work in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning.

By the end of this phase, I had learned how ER diagrams, SQL, Primary Keys, and Foreign Keys work together to create an efficient database system. This knowledge became very useful in the later stages of our project.
